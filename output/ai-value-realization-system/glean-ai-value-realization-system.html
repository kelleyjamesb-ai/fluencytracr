Page 1 · Whole AI value proposition

Glean’s value system needs three layers: model value, measure readiness, and validate behavior.

ROIbot, the AI Fluency Instrument, and FluencyTracr should not compete. Together they create a stronger value-realization motion: estimate value, understand human readiness, observe aggregate behavior, then validate business outcomes.

Business priority

What outcome matters?

Start with the customer’s goal: growth, cycle time, support quality, onboarding, cost avoidance, risk, or capacity.

ROIbot

Value model

Packages assumptions, time saved, cost avoided, capacity created, and scenario-based value estimates.

AI Fluency Instrument

Human readiness

Measures confidence, usage quality, behavior change, leadership reinforcement, capability growth, intent, and perceived impact.

FluencyTracr

Observed work evidence

Measures aggregate velocity, depth, breadth, quality, reliability, trust gaps, and whether AI use is becoming embedded in workflows.

Outcome validation

Business proof path

Connects to customer-owned systems of record: Salesforce, ServiceNow, Jira, Zendesk, Workday, support systems, and finance context.

Usage + Fluency + Workflow Evidence + Outcome Context = Defensible Value Investigation
What this solves The value motion stops being only “hours saved” and becomes “where AI changes work, creates capacity, and warrants value investigation.”
Why it matters It protects Glean from overclaiming while giving customers a clearer path from adoption to work redesign and business impact.
Best positioning ROIbot is the modeling layer. The AI Fluency Instrument is the human-change layer. FluencyTracr is the behavioral evidence layer.
Executive takeaway: The full value proposition is not “AI saved time.” It is “AI created capacity, the organization was ready to use it, behavior changed in observable workflows, and the evidence is strong enough to investigate business impact.”

Page 2 · What each component contributes

The pieces are complementary if each one owns a clear job.

The cleanest operating model is a shared evidence chain. Each component contributes a different type of signal, and the Value Playbook governs what claims can be made.

Component Primary job Signal type What it can say What it cannot say alone
ROIbot Value modeling and packaging Assumptions, time saved, cost avoided, recapture, scenario estimates “Here is the modeled value hypothesis.” “This value was realized.”
AI Fluency Instrument Human readiness and behavior-change measurement Confidence, usage quality, behavior change, leadership reinforcement, capability growth, intent, perceived impact “People are more or less ready to change how they work with AI.” “The business impact happened.”
FluencyTracr / AI Work Evidence Observed aggregate AI behavior evidence Velocity, breadth, Depth Repertoire, quality, reliability, trust evidence gaps, scale readiness zones “AI use is broad, repeated, reliable, shallow, frictioned, or held.” “AI caused ROI or productivity lift.”
Metrics Library KPI selection by function and use case Sales, support, engineering, HR, marketing, product, CS metrics “These are plausible business metrics to test.” “These metrics moved because of Glean.”
Process Library Current-state and future-state workflow design Workflow steps, handoffs, bottlenecks, time assumptions “Here is where work could be redesigned.” “The redesign was adopted and sustained.”
Value Playbook Governance and operating model Evidence tiers, baselines, claim rules, handoff motion, review cadence “This is what we are allowed to claim at this stage.” “The data is complete.”
Business systems of record Outcome validation Ticket deflection, MTTR, win rate, cycle time, onboarding, cost, quality “This business metric moved.” “Glean caused the movement without an approved design.”
The combined narrative
  • ROIbot creates a value hypothesis.
  • AI Fluency Instrument shows whether people are ready and changing.
  • FluencyTracr shows whether behavior is changing in aggregate workflows.
  • Business systems validate whether outcomes moved.
The governance rule
  • Survey-only means directional.
  • Telemetry-only means evidence-backed behavior.
  • System-of-record alignment means stronger value investigation.
  • ROI requires customer-owned assumptions and evidence tiers.
How to describe this simply: ROIbot estimates the value. The AI Fluency Instrument measures whether people are ready to realize it. FluencyTracr checks whether the work behavior is actually changing. Customer systems validate the business outcome.

Page 3 · What is missing based on the PPTs

The PPTs already define the ambition. The missing work is evidence discipline and operational stitching.

The materials are strong on value narrative, workflow examples, metric libraries, and ROI modeling. The remaining gaps are mostly about proof, attribution, baselines, and where each signal lives in the operating model.

Partial

Evidence tiers

Need a clear distinction between estimate, evidence-backed hypothesis, and customer-validated impact.

Partial

Baseline capture

Need pre-go-live baseline for workflow time, KPI state, fluency, and current-state process pain.

Held

Outcome joins

Need governed connection to Salesforce, ServiceNow, Jira, Zendesk, Workday, or other systems of record.

Partial

Capacity recapture

Need to show how saved time is redeployed into named higher-value work, not just counted as hours.

Started

Workflow evidence

FluencyTracr can help test whether AI use is broad, repeated, and embedded in aggregate workflows.

Held

Trust attribution

Verification, feedback, and citation behavior exist, but need stricter parent attribution before governed trust claims.

Held

Reusable leverage

Need better skill/workflow metadata before claiming reusable organizational leverage from skills, agents, or templates.

Partial

Segmentation

Need approved aggregate joins for department, role, tenure, level, region, or behavior bands without ranking risk.
